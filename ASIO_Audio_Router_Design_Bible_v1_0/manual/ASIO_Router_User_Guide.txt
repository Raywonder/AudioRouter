ASIO AUDIO ROUTER USER GUIDE

1. What ASIO Audio Router Does

ASIO Audio Router lets you route audio from apps, microphones, devices, screen readers, TTS engines, communications apps, OBS, and future VoiceLink/TCast workflows.

It is designed so blind users can operate it with NVDA, JAWS, Narrator, and keyboard-only navigation.

2. Important Default Channels

1/2: Main Out.
61/62: Screen Reader Bus.
63/64: TTS Bus.
65/66: Communications Bus.
67/68: Emergency Accessibility Bus.

3. Screen Reader Audio

Screen reader audio is protected by default.

By default, NVDA, JAWS, and Narrator should go to your selected headphones or accessibility output and should not be included in recordings or streams.

You may change this in Settings, but the system should never leak screen reader audio into OBS by accident.

4. TTS Audio

TTS from OpenClaw, AI assistants, VoiceLink assistants, or future agents can use the TTS Bus.

By default, TTS goes where you choose and stays separate from screen readers and program audio.

5. Smart I/O

When you plug in a device, ASIO Audio Router can ask what kind of device it is and remember it.

Examples:
- Microphone.
- Headphones.
- Audio Interface.
- Virtual Device.
- Accessibility Device.

6. Smart Buffer

Smart Buffer watches CPU, glitches, plugin load, and timing. It can raise the buffer when audio is unstable and lower it after the system becomes stable.

7. OBS Use

Use separate tracks instead of one desktop audio track.

Example:
Chrome to 3/4.
Mic to 7/8.
VoiceLink to 65/66.
Screen reader stays headphones only.

8. Emergency Mute

Press Ctrl+Shift+M to mute outputs quickly during feedback or unexpected routing.

9. Keyboard Basics

Tab and Shift+Tab move between controls.
Arrow keys adjust sliders.
Enter activates.
Space toggles.
Ctrl+Shift+P opens the command palette.
Ctrl+Shift+M activates emergency mute.

10. Troubleshooting

If you hear feedback, press Ctrl+Shift+M.
If a device disappears, reconnect it and allow Smart I/O to restore the profile.
If your screen reader is heard on stream, check Screen Reader Bus settings and confirm include in stream is off.
